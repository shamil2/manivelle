## Design Context

### Users
- **High-end Homeowners:** Seeking unique, sustainable statement pieces for their homes.
- **Interior Designers:** Looking for bespoke, artisanal furniture for high-concept projects.
- **Eco-conscious Consumers:** Valuing circular economy, recycled materials, and artisanal longevity.
- **Workshop Attendees:** DIY enthusiasts interested in learning the craft of cartonnage.

### Brand Personality
- **Artisanal:** Human, imperfect, tactile, and highly skillful.
- **Eco-Responsible:** Resourceful, respectful of raw materials, and circular by design.
- **Sophisticated:** Editorial, high-end, and deeply intentional in every choice.
- **Voice:** Knowledgeable but humble, like a master craftsperson sharing their passion.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone:** "Modern Artisanal." Evoking a sun-drenched workshop in rural France.
- **Visuals:** Warm natural lighting, tactile cardboard textures, "raw" edges, and high-contrast editorial layouts.
- **Colors:** Warm Parchment (#F9F9F5) background, Ink Black (#2D2D2A) text, and Terracotta (#D97757) accents.
- **Typography:** *Instrument Serif* (Editorial Display) paired with *Geist* or *Outfit* (Modern Body).

### Design Principles
1. **Material First:** Celebrate the cardboard. Use textures, structural details, and raw edges as primary design elements rather than hiding them.
2. **Skillful Asymmetry:** Avoid generic "template" grids. Use unbalanced compositions, overlapping elements, and generous, intentional white space.
3. **Physicality in Motion:** Animations should feel like physical objects being moved. Weighted, deliberate, and smooth (prefer `ease-out-expo`).
4. **Editorial Hierarchy:** Treat the page like a premium magazine. Large display type, intentional use of italics, and clear separation of "Technical Specs" vs "Artistic Story."
5. **Human Touch:** Incorporate subtle "imperfections" (irregular borders, hand-drawn-style underlines) to signal that a human, not a machine, built this.
